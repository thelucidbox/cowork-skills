## Citation Rules

### Every Claim Needs Citation
- Statistics: Must cite source
- Quotes: Must cite speaker and source
- Facts: Must cite source
- Opinions: Must attribute

### Citation Format
Use footnotes: [^1], [^2], etc.

### Reference Format
```
[^1]: Author/Organization. "Title." Publication/Site. Date. URL (accessed Date).
```

---

## Source Evaluation

### Credibility Factors
- **Author expertise**: Known in field?
- **Publication quality**: Peer-reviewed, reputable outlet?
- **Date**: Recent for fast-moving topics?
- **Methodology**: For studies, sound approach?
- **Bias**: Commercial interest? Ideological stance?

### Credibility Rating
- **High**: Academic, established institution, methodology clear
- **Medium**: Reputable publication, potential bias noted
- **Low**: Unknown source, clear bias, no methodology

---

## Fact vs. Opinion

### Clearly Distinguish
- **Fact**: Verifiable, cite source
- **Opinion**: Attribute: "According to [source]..."
- **Analysis**: Label as your synthesis

### Language
- Facts: "Research shows..." "Data indicates..."
- Opinions: "Source argues..." "Expert believes..."
- Analysis: "This suggests..." "The implication is..."

---
